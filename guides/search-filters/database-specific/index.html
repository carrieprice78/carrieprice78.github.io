Database-Specific Searches

PubMed

PubMed has a number of subject filters built in and can be called by appending a search by typing the appropriate words and [sb]. As of 2020, these appear to no longer be maintained. I suggest using them as guidance.

  • AIDS: use aids [sb]
  • Bioethics: use bioethics [sb]
  • Cancer: use cancer [sb]
  • Complementary Medicine: use cam [sb]
  • Developmental and Reproductive Toxicology: use dart [sb]
  • Dietary Supplements: use dietsuppl [sb]
  • History of Medicine: use history [sb]
  • Space Life Sciences: use space [sb]
  • Systematic Reviews: use systematic [sb]
  • Toxicology: use tox [sbu]
  • Veterinary Science: use veterinary [sb]

Miscellaneous

  • Flinders Filters and CareSearch Filters follow an established methodology designed to be objective and minimize bias
  • Health Information Research Unit at McMaster University has written various database filters for therapy, diagnosis, review, prognosis, causation, etiology, economics, cost, clinical prediction guides, qualitative, and more for MEDLINE, Embase, and PsycINFO
  • ISSG at InterTASC Search Filter Resource has a number of searches for different databases
  • Search Filters for Various Databases from the University of Texas School of Public Health
